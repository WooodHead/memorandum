## 2.1

### Definition complete measure
* $(S, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$,
    * probability space

$\mu$ is said to be complete if

$$
    N \in \mathcal{A},
    \
    \mi(N) = 0,
    \
    \Rightarrow
    \
    \forall S \subseteq N,
    \
    S \in \mathcal{A}
$$
